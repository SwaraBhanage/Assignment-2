# BioRender Protocol: Near-Infrared (NIR) Optical Imaging of Aβ Plaques

**Author:** Swara Bhanage  
**Institution:** Institute of Bioinformatics and Biotechnology (IBB), SPPU  
**Reference:** Luo, Z., et al. (2021). "Optical Imaging of Amyloid-β Plaques: From Molecular Probes to Precision Diagnosis." *Biosensors*.

---

### 📌 Project Overview
This infographic maps the pathological progression of Alzheimer’s Disease (AD) and the subsequent technological solution: high-resolution optical imaging. The workflow transitions from healthy synaptic physiology to the aggregation of amyloid-beta (Aβ) plaques, their neurotoxic impact on the brain, and the final use of Near-Infrared (NIR) fluorescent probes for early-stage diagnosis.

**Canvas Specifications:**
* **Tool:** BioRender
* **Dimensions:** 1200 x 628 px (Horizontal 4-Zone Grid)
* **Design Rule:** Visual continuity is maintained by using the same "Pink Neuron" and "Blue Capsule" icons across all zones to represent the sequential progression of the disease.

---

### 🛠️ Step-by-Step Construction Guide

#### ZONE 1: PHYSIOLOGY (Normal Synaptic Transmission)
* **Concept:** Establishing the baseline of healthy neuronal communication.
* **BioRender Elements:** Presynaptic Neuron, Postsynaptic Neuron, Vesicles, Neurotransmitters, Receptors.
* **Instructions:**
    1. Place the **Presynaptic Neuron** on the left and the **Postsynaptic Neuron** on the right.
    2. Use a **Zoom-In Circle** to enlarge the synaptic cleft.
    3. Inside the synapse, place small blue circles (Neurotransmitters) being released from vesicles and binding to purple receptors.
    4. Label the zone: **"Normal Synaptic Transmission."**

#### ZONE 2: PATHOLOGY (Dysregulated Aβ Cleavage)
* **Concept:** The biochemical "molecular error" that initiates plaque formation.
* **BioRender Elements:** Presynaptic Membrane, Amyloid Precursor Protein (APP), **Scissors** (Enzymatic Cleavage), Aβ Monomers.
* **Instructions:**
    1. Place a large purple blob (APP) on the presynaptic membrane.
    2. Position a **Scissors** icon labeled **"beta-gamma secretases"** at the cleavage site.
    3. Draw a motion arrow showing the released Aβ "pills" (monomers) aggregating into a small stack.
    4. Label the product: **"amyloid beta plaques."**

#### ZONE 3: DEGENERATION (Neurotoxicity & Synaptic Failure)
* **Concept:** The systemic damage caused by plaque accumulation and immune response.
* **BioRender Elements:** Stacked Aβ Plaque, **Activated Microglia**, **Broken Signal** icon, Fragmented Neuron.
* **Instructions:**
    1. Re-use the plaque clump from Zone 2, placing it directly in the synaptic cleft.
    2. Position spiky yellow icons (**Activated Microglia**) touching the plaque to represent neuroinflammation.
    3. Place a **Red "Broken Lightning Bolt"** over the synapse to signify signal blockage.
    4. Modify the postsynaptic neuron using the **"Transparency"** and **"Eraser"** tools to show it fragmenting and "dying."
    5. Label the zone: **"Neurotoxicity and synaptic dysfunction."**

#### ZONE 4: DETECTION (NIR Optical Imaging Solution)
* **Concept:** Visualizing the invisible pathology using the paper’s optical methodology.
* **BioRender Elements:** Confocal Optical Path (Laser, Mirror, Pinhole, PMT), Aβ Plaque (with Glow), NIR Probes.
* **Instructions:**
    1. Re-create the simplified **Confocal Microscope Path**:
        * **Laser Source:** A red line representing NIR light (λex ≈ 630 nm).
        * **Dichroic Mirror:** A slanted line that reflects the laser down but lets the signal pass up.
    2. **The Sample:** Place the plaque clump at the bottom of the path.
    3. **The Probe:** Add small neon-green hexagons (**Binding of CRANAD-2 Probe**) binding to the plaque.
    4. **Fluorescence:** Apply a bright **Aura/Glow** effect to the plaque to represent the emission signal traveling back to the **Detector (PMT)**.

### 🎨 Design & Formatting Rules

* **Scientific Accuracy:** Ensure the peptide is consistently labeled as **Aβ (Amyloid-beta)** and not "alpha beta."
* **Color-Coding:** The color of the Aβ monomers in Zone 2 must perfectly match the color of the aggregate in Zone 3 and the imaging sample in Zone 4.
* **Alignment:** Zone headers should be top-aligned. Use BioRender’s **"Group"** function to ensure the complex optical path in Zone 4 can be moved as a single unit.
* **Reference Footer:** Include the citation *Luo, Z., et al. (2021)* in 8pt font at the bottom right of the canvas.
